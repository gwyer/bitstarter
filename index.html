One of the most difficult aspects of raising responsible, independent children is getting them to do what parents, teachers and caregivers want them to do. There are generally two extreme approaches and many gray areas in between. 1. Coercive approach: determine your child's current currency and leverage it to induce them to help around the house and behave at school and in restaurants. Often this takes the form of an iPad or other electronic device whisked out at appropriate moments. 2. Authoritarian approach: shout and raise a tantrum until task is completed and hopefully behaviour improves.

The tool will allow kids and parents to set their goals on a daily and weekly basis. A public page will allow the kids / parents / teachers to provide feedback without need to pass notes and consolidate daily scores. (This may also be done using Twitter).

The reward system is based on the child's currency (negotiated with parent), for example a trip to zoo or purchase of a toy. Points are allocated for the completion of each task or good behaviour (customizable points for each task).
